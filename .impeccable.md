## Design Context

### Users
Independent artists and rappers shopping online for beats. They're browsing on their own time — phone or laptop — comparing producers, listening for the right sound, and deciding whether to trust someone with their music. The job to be done: find a beat that fits their vision and feel confident the producer is the real deal.

### Brand Personality
**Confident. Personal. Legit.**

KJYOUCRAZY is not a marketplace — it's a producer with a name and verified credits. The voice is direct and self-assured, never hype-bro loud. Think: an artist who's placed records with GloRilla and Shenseea and doesn't need to shout about it — the receipts speak. Warm enough to feel human, sharp enough to feel professional.

Emotional goal: visitors should leave thinking *"this producer is legit"* — not overwhelmed, not underwhelmed.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Dark, cinematic, and personal.** Pure black (`#000`) with near-white text (`#f5f5f7`). Minimal but not cold — the monochrome palette is intentional and should stay dominant.

- **Accent color:** Warm amber/gold tones (`~#c8a96e` or `~#d4a853`) for moments that need a pulse — verified badges, active states, featured callouts, price highlights. Not neon, not red, not corporate blue.
- **Typography:** Bebas Neue for headlines (impact, personality), Inter for everything UI (precision, readability). Montserrat available as a mid-weight option for pull quotes or labels.
- **Motion:** Purposeful and restrained — stagger reveals, subtle parallax, glass morphism nav. Never distracting or theme-park.
- **Texture:** Very low-key — thin white/5–10 borders, radial glows, frosted glass. No gradients that compete with content.

**Anti-references:** flashy beat store aesthetics (excessive glow, neon, cluttered), overly corporate/cold agency sites, generic SaaS minimalism.

### Design Principles

1. **Credibility over hype.** Let the placements and the music do the talking. Every design choice should reinforce trust, not excitement for its own sake.

2. **Personal, not anonymous.** This is a producer with an identity — not a marketplace. Typography, voice, and layout should feel like a person behind it, not a platform.

3. **Monochrome as a feature.** Black/white is the brand. Warm accent colors earn their place; they should be used sparingly and only when they reinforce meaning (status, action, emphasis).

4. **Motion serves clarity.** Animations should aid comprehension or guide attention — never run just to show off. If it can be removed without losing meaning, remove it.

5. **Mobile-first credibility.** Artists are on their phones. Every critical element — beat cards, player, licensing info, checkout — must feel polished at 375px, not just adapted from desktop.
